Wooden Utensils

-Utensils for cooking are typically made from hardwoods, such as teak, oak, sapele, and meranti.Other types of wood that can be used for kitchen utensils include bloodwood, Osage orange, black walnut, hard maple, jatoba, cherry, and mesquite. Wooden utensils have natural qualities that mean they handle heat better, do not become too hot in use and have antibacterial properties that help trap and eliminate germs and bacteria, making it safer to use and more sanitary.Maple is a versatile and popular choice for crafting kitchen utensils due to its dense and durable nature, making it an ideal option for food-safe wood products. The benefits of using maple for kitchen utensils are numerous.
